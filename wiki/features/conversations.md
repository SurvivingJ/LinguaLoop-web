# Conversation Generation

## Purpose

Conversations are simulated dialogues generated by LLM. They serve as a realistic corpus from which key words, phrases, and grammatical patterns can be extracted. Conversations are the raw material for Language Packs — they provide authentic-feeling language samples in controlled topic domains.

## User Story

From the system perspective: when an admin generates a Language Pack on a topic, the first step is generating a set of simulated conversations. These conversations model how native speakers would discuss the topic in various scenarios.

From the learner perspective: conversations appear within Language Packs as reading material, with comprehension questions testing understanding.

## How It Works

1. A topic and language are specified (e.g., "Soccer" in Japanese).
2. The conversation generation pipeline creates personas, scenarios, and then full dialogues:
   - **Persona Designer** — creates character profiles with backgrounds and speech styles
   - **Scenario Planner** — designs conversation situations and goals
   - **Dialogue Generator** — produces the actual conversation text
   - **Quality Checker** — validates naturalness and language accuracy
   - **Conversation Analyzer** — extracts key vocabulary and grammar
3. Generated conversations are stored and made available for:
   - Corpus analysis (collocation extraction, frequency analysis)
   - Language Pack content (reading passages, comprehension questions)
   - Vocabulary extraction (key words and phrases)

## Constraints & Edge Cases

- Conversations should feel natural and appropriate for the target age tier (see [[decisions/ADR-003-age-tiers]]).
- Different conversation styles (casual, business, academic) should be representable.
- Language-specific conventions (honorifics in Japanese, formal/informal in many languages) must be respected.

## Business Rules

- Conversations are generated at pack creation time, not on demand.
- Quality checking is automated but may flag items for human review.
